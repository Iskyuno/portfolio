Capstone · 2025

Arduino Self-Lifting Motorcycle Platform

A BS Computer Engineering capstone: an automated flood-protection platform that keeps a parked motorcycle safe when water levels rise. When flooding is detected, a pneumatic scissor-lift raises the vehicle clear of the water, while integrated safety and security subsystems keep it protected the whole time. The entire system is sensed, sequenced, and controlled by Arduino.

// Four integrated subsystems

  • Pneumatic scissor-lift — raises the motorcycle above flood level on demand.
  • Flood / water-level sensing — triggers the lift automatically as water rises.
  • Pressurized water rinser — cleans the vehicle of floodwater residue.
  • GSM anti-theft detection — sends SMS alerts to the owner on tamper events.

Field deployment · iOne Resources

DepEd SWIP Network Deployment

As part of iOne Resources' work on the DepEd School Wi-Fi Internet Program (SWIP), I helped deploy and configure TP-Link Omada networking systems across multiple school sites — taking each site from cabling and access-point mounting through to a benchmarked, secured, and managed network.

// What I delivered

  • Validated 40 Mbps benchmarks — confirmed throughput targets at each site.
  • Captive portals & voucher auth — controlled, accountable guest access.
  • Content filtering — safe, education-appropriate browsing for schools.
  • Structured cabling & PoE+ APs — clean, reliable Wi-Fi 6 coverage.
